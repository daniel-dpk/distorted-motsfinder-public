Detailed Description

Brill-Lindquist 3-metric of two non-rotating, non-spinning black holes.

This metric due to Brill and Lindquist [1] describes, as implemented here, two non-spinning black holes at a moment of time-symmetry before evolving to a head-on collision.

The metric itself is conformally flat, i.e.

\[ g = \psi^4\ \delta , \]

where \( \delta \) is the flat 3-metric and the conformal factor is obtained from

\[ \psi = 1 + \frac{m_1}{2 r_1} + \frac{m_2}{2 r_2}. \]

Here, \( m_i \) are mass parameters (not the ADM masses of the two asymptotic ends) for the individual black holes and \( r_i \) the coordinate distances to the two punctures, i.e.

\[ r_i = \Vert \vec x_i \Vert, \quad\mbox{where}\quad \vec x_i := \vec x - \vec c_i. \]

In this class, we choose to put the two punctures along one of the axes at equal coordinate distances from the coordinate origin. For example for the z-axis, we have \(\vec c_1 = -\vec c_2 = (0, 0, d/2)\), where d can be specified upon construction.

Notes

Derivatives of the conformal factor are computed using

\begin{eqnarray*} \partial_i\psi &=& - \frac{m_1 x_1^i}{2r_1^3} - \frac{m_2 x_2^i}{2r_2^3} \\ \partial_i\partial_j\psi &=& - \frac{m_1 \delta_{ij}}{2r_1^3} - \frac{m_2 \delta_{ij}}{2r_2^3} + \frac{3}{2} \frac{m_1 x_1^i x_1^j}{r_1^5} + \frac{3}{2} \frac{m_2 x_2^i x_2^j}{r_2^5}. \end{eqnarray*}

References

[1] Brill, Dieter R., and Richard W. Lindquist. "Interaction energy in geometrostatics." Physical Review 131.1 (1963): 471.

Construct a Brill-Lindquist 3-metric.

The ADM mass of the metric (specifically, the ADM mass of the asymptotic end \( \Vert \vec x \Vert \to \infty\)) will be m1+m2.

Parameters
d(float) Distance parameter. This represents the coordinate distance of the two punctures. If it is chosen low enough, a common horizon forms which is usually interpreted as the two black holes having merged into a single black hole. Separate inner MOTSs may still exist. The value at which a common horizon forms for equal mass black holes has been determined to low accuracy in [1] and with higher accuracy in [2], see Table 2.2 therein. It reads approximately \(d/m \approx 1.532395\).
m1(float) Mass parameter of first black hole. Default is 1.
m2(float) Mass parameter of second black hole. Default is 1.
axis({'x', 'y', 'z'}, optional) Symmetry axis on which to place the two punctures. Default is ‘'z’`.

References

[2] Lages, Norbert. Apparent Horizons and Marginally Trapped Surfaces in Numerical General Relativity. Diss. PhD thesis, University of Jena, 2010.

Compute the value of psi at a given point.

Parameters
point3D point at which to evaluate psi.
derivativesWhether to compute the derivatives in addition to the value of psi itself. Default is False, i.e. compute only psi itself.
max_derivMaximum derivative order to return.
Returns
If derivatives==False, returns just the value psi(point). Otherwise, returns a tuple of derivatives of psi, starting at 0 up to max_deriv (inclusive). Each derivative of order > 0 is a nested sequence with indices i1, i2, ... meaning \(\partial_{i_1}\partial_{i_2}\psi\).
